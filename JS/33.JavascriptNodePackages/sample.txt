The Battle of Qadisiyyah, which took place in 636 AD, was an important battle between the Islamic Caliphate and the Sassanid Empire. This battle is considered a decisive phase of the Arab conquests. In the battle, the Muslim army defeated the large forces of the Sassanids and captured a large part of Iraq. The Battle of Qadisiyyah played a significant role in the spread of Islam and the weakening of the Sassanids. This victory led to the Muslims gaining power in the Middle East and changed the political map of the region. The outcome of the battle is considered an important stage in the expansion of the Islamic Caliphate.
